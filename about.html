About Me



Hi and welcome to my professional website! My name is David Fasina and I am a Computer Engineering student at Texas A&M University Class of 2022. I was born in Nigeria and moved at the age of 1 to grow up in Texas (from 9 years in H-Town to 8 years in A-town). I am passionate about hardware and software development with direct interests in frontend software design and embedded systems. I have spent time in High School and College working in retail specific roles and that consumer focused-based mindset carries over in the code and systems that I currently design and hope to design in the future. One of my current team projects mentioned on my portfolio page is named StuddyBuddy, and its user focus makes it enjoyable to develop.

My first experience with a full PC was a Gateway branded laptop me and my sister shared when I was 6. The extent of my use at that age was spending hours playing flash games on Miniclip and Webkinz and I partially owe my passion to those websites. The rest of my debt is owed to my mom for the sacrifices she made to enable me. My dad passed away when I was 4 years old and he made a large sacrifice to relocate our family to the United States for work and I am truly grateful for this. Despite growing up in a single-parented household, my mom never let me or my sister feel like we were missing anything from an ideal childhood, and I am beyond blessed for that. As a result, in my professional work I hope to be able to give back to my community and expand the privileges that I have been afforded in my life. In addition, I wish to expand viewpoints and diversity in the workplace so that all the voices in our communities can be heard.
